Start broad, then go deep
Begin with an overview prompt like “Give me a structured overview of [topic] — key concepts, subtopics, and what I should understand first.” This gives you a map before diving in.

Use one long conversation, not many short ones
Stay in the same chat so Claude builds on what’s already been discussed

Ask Claude to act as a teacher or expert
Set the tone early: “Explain this to me like I’m a beginner” or “Assume I have a background in X.” This calibrates the depth.

Go subtopic by subtopic
Don’t try to learn everything at once. Ask Claude to break the topic into sections, then explore each one before moving on.
Challenge and verify
Ask follow-up questions like “What are the counterarguments to this?” or “Where do experts disagree?” This builds critical understanding, not just surface knowledge.
Use Claude’s Research feature for current info
If your topic needs up-to-date sources, use the Deep Research feature (the magnifying glass icon in the input bar). It searches the web and synthesizes findings into a report.
Summarize as you go
Periodically ask “Summarize what we’ve covered so far” — this helps consolidate learning and gives you notes to save.
